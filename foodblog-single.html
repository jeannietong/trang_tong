CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIE RECIPE

  • by: Trang Tong

  • date: Jan 24, 2021

Chocolate chip cookie is one of my favorites. I have tried many different versions of this cookie and I have learned one thing is that a tasty cookie has to meet 4 essentials: ensure the crispy, crunchy, not burnt and especially not too sweet or bitter. I quite focus on the appearance of the cookie, a cookie that is meticulously cared for from appearance to taste will definitely attract more people.

I started baking cookies on November, 2020 - during the quarantine time. Up to now, I got some experience as well as tips to share with you about my baking journey. I'm not sure the recipe suits for everyone's taste but I hope this helps you a bit in making your own cookies.

This batch of cookies is the most perfect version for me after many careful trials, from how to adjust the volume and recipe to how to choose the type of chocolate chip. According to my experience, with the simplest ingredients, the easiest way, we have been able to make chocolate cookies with eye-catching appearance, moderate sweetness, buttery aroma and crunch.

With this recipe, I made 20 cookies (6cm in diameter). Timing: 1 hour

☀︎ INGREDIENTS:

• 150g all-purpose flour

• 1 tsp baking soda

• 1/2 tsp salt

• 60g granulated sugar

• 50g brown sugar

• 120g unsalted butter (keep butter at room temperature)

• 1 large egg

• 1 tsp vanilla extract

• 80g chocolate chips (Organic 70% dark chocolate chips of Artisan Kettle or M&M chocolate)

☀︎ PREPARE DOUGH MIXTURE:

- Sift all-purpose flour, salt, and baking soda into a bowl and use a spatula to mix.

- In another bowl, using a mixer on medium speed, beat the butter with the sugar (granulated and brown sugar) until smooth (about 3-5 minutes).

- Continue to add vanilla extract and egg, beating on low speed until well-combined (about 2 minutes).

- Gradually add the flour mixture above and mix well until the mixture is smooth.

NOTE: Do not beat for too long because it will make the mixture dry and disjointed, the cookies will not be crispy when it comes out of the oven.

- Add chocolate chips and use spatula to mix well.

★ At this step, the flour mixture will be very smooth and light brown, depending on the type of brown sugar used, cookies will have different color tones.

- Cover and chill the dough in the fridge for about 30 minutes to let it rise and ferment.

NOTE: Do not leave the dough for too long at low heat as it will harden the dough.

☀︎ BAKING:

- Preheat oven to 170°C.

- Prepare a baking tray and line it with parchment paper.

- Shape the dough into equal parts. I used a spring spoon to scoop ice cream (4cm in diameter) to create equal proportions.

NOTE: The size of cookie is very important when baking because if you bake cookies of different sizes, the small/thin cookies will burn while the large/thick ones will not be crispy.

NOTE: No need to roll out the dough, when baking, the cake will automatically flatten into a circle with a diameter of 6-7cm.

- Place them tray, the distance between the dough balls is 5cm.

- Bake at 170°C for 15 minutes until golden brown.

- After baking for the specified amount of time, turn off the oven and leave cookies for another 5 minutes while the oven is still hot.

In my experience, in order for the cookies to be really crispy, after baking, I lowered the oven to 100°C and put them back in and dried for about 10 minutes. Remove cookies from the oven and place on a rack to cool. Freshly baked cookies will still be soft, when completely cooled, they will be more crispy and delicious.

★ Store cookies in an airtight container in a dry place or in the refrigerator. If cookies are hard or mushy, they can be reheated at 100°C for 5-8 minutes. Using cookies within 1 week, leaving it for a long time will reduce the quality.
